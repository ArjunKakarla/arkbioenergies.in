Why 2G Ethanol?

2G ethanol uses non-food agricultural residues, avoids food vs fuel conflicts and reduces greenhouse gas emissions compared to fossil fuels.

Policy Tailwinds

Supported by the JI-VAN scheme and state incentives. Ethanol blending mandates create guaranteed demand for off-takers.

The Solution ‚Äî Ballari 2G Ethanol Plant

A fully integrated plant that converts rice straw & husk to ethanol using pre-treatment, enzymatic hydrolysis, fermentation and distillation. The process also produces lignin for power generation and other by-products.

Step 1

Feedstock Collection

Sourcing rice straw & husk from local farms via aggregators and MoUs.

Step 2

Pre-treatment

Breaking down lignocellulosic structure for enzyme access.

Step 3

Hydrolysis & Fermentation

Enzymatic conversion to sugars followed by fermentation to ethanol.

Step 4

Distillation & Purification

Produce fuel-grade ethanol meeting industry specs.

Step 5

By-products

Lignin retained for captive power; CO2 purification and other streams monetized.

Step 6

Dispatch

Quality-assured ethanol supplied to OMCs and industrial buyers.
