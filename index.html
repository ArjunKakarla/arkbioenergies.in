Our Strategic Vision

Ark Bio Energies is not just building a factory; we are building an ecosystem. Our vision aligns with the Prime Minister's "Atmanirbhar Bharat" initiative, targeting energy independence through bio-resources.

01 Triple Bottom Line

We operate on the principle of People, Planet, and Profit. We generate revenue for farmers (People), reduce carbon emissions by 95% compared to fossil fuels (Planet), and deliver robust returns to our stakeholders (Profit).

02 Solving Stubble Burning

The Ballari region generates vast amounts of rice straw. Currently, much of this is burned, causing severe air pollution. We monetize this waste, turning a hazardous liability into a valuable asset.

03 Energy Security

India currently imports over 80% of its crude oil. By producing 2G Ethanol domestically, we directly contribute to reducing the national import bill and forex outflow.

The E20 Mandate

The Government of India has advanced the target for 20% ethanol blending in petrol (E20) to 2025. Current capacity is insufficient to meet this target, creating a massive supply vacuum for new entrants.

Guaranteed Offtake

Oil Marketing Companies (IOCL, BPCL, HPCL) provide 100% guaranteed offtake agreements for ethanol produced in India, eliminating sales risk entirely.

Price Stability

The procurement price is fixed by the CCEA (Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs) annually, protecting producers from daily market volatility.

Proprietary Technology

Advanced 2G Fractionation

Unlike First Generation (1G) ethanol, which uses food sources like sugarcane or corn, our Second Generation (2G) technology utilizes lignocellulosic biomass—specifically rice straw.

We employ a state-of-the-art Steam Explosion pretreatment followed by specialized enzymatic hydrolysis. This ensures we extract maximum fermentable sugars from the tough agricultural residue, achieving industry-leading yields.

1

Feedstock Preparation

Baling and shredding of rice straw, followed by dust removal to ensure clean input for the reactor.

2

Steam Explosion

High-pressure thermal treatment breaks down the rigid lignin structure, exposing cellulose.

3

Enzymatic Hydrolysis

Specialized enzyme cocktails convert the exposed cellulose into fermentable glucose sugars.

4

Fermentation & Recovery

Yeast converts sugars to ethanol, which is then distilled to 99.9% purity fuel-grade ethanol.
